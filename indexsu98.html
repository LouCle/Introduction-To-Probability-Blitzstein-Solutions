3.1.2 problem 2

(a)
Since the trials are independent, the probability that the first k − 1 trials fail is (1 2)k−1, and the probability that the k-th trial is successful is 1 2. Thus, for k ≥ 1,

P(X = k) = (1 2)k−1 ∗ 1 2.

(b)
This problem reduces to part a once a trial is performed. Whatever it’s outcome, we label it failure and proceed to perform more trials until the opposite outcome is observed. Thus, for k ≥ 2,

P(X = k) = (1 2)k−2 ∗ 1 2.
