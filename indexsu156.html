4.2.8 problem 33

Suppose w = r = 1. The white ball is equally likely to be any of the w + b balls. Also, note that the event k-th drawn ball is the white ball is equivalent to the event k − 1 black balls are drawn until the white ball is drawn. Thus, for X ∼NHGeom(1,n − 1, 1), P(X = k) = P(k + 1-th drawn ball is white) = 1 n for 0 ≤ k ≤ n − 1.

P(X = k) = (1+k−1 1−1)( 1+n−1−1−k 1−1) (1+n−1 1) = 1 n
