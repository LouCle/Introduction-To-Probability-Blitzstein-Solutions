1.3.9 problem 38

(a)
There are 12 choices of seats for Tyron and Cersei so that they sit next to each other (11 cases, where they take i − 1 and i positions and 1 case, where they take 1 and 12th position, because table is round). Tyron can sit to the left or to the right of Cersei. The remaining 10 people can be ordered in 10! ways, so the answer is 24 × 10! 12! = 2 11

(b)
There are (12 2) choices of seats to be assigned to Tyron and Cersei, but only 12 choices where they sit next to each other. Since every assignment of seats is equally likely the answer is 12 (12 2) = 2 11
