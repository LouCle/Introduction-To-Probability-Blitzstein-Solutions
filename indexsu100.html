3.1.4 problem 4

To show that F(x) is a CDF, we need to show that F is increasing, right-continuous, and converges to 0 and 1 in the limits.

The first condition is true since ⌊x⌋ is increasing.

Since lim ⁡ x→a+F(x) = F(a) when a ∈ ℕ by the definition of F(x), the second condition is satisfied.

lim ⁡ x→∞F(x) = 1 by the definition of F(x), and also, by definition, lim ⁡ x→−∞F(x) = 0. Thus, the third condition is satisfied, and F(x) is a CDF.

The PMF F corresponds to is

P(X = k) = 1 n

for 1 ≤ k ≤ n and 0 everywhere else.
