4.3.15 problem 56

(a)
Let Ij be the indicator variable for shots j to j + 6 being successful. The total number of succesful, consecutive, 7 shots is X = ∑ ⁡ i=1nI j. Then, E(X) = ∑ i=1nE(I j) = ∑ i=1nP(I j = 1) = ∑ i=1np7 = np7

(b)
Thinking of each block of 7 shots as a single trial with probability p7 of success, let Y ∼Geom(p7) be the number of failed 7-block shots taken until the first succesful 7-shot block. Then, E(X) = 7(1 + E(Y )) = 7 + 7 − 7p7 p7 = 7p7 + 7 − 7p7 p7 = 7 p7
