2.1.22 problem 29

Let Gi be the event that the i-th child is a girl. Let Ci be the event that the i-th child has property C.

P(G1 ∩ G2|(G1 ∩ C1) ∪ (G2 ∩ C2)) = 0.25(2p−p2) 0.5p+0.5p−0.25p2 = 0.5(2−p) 2−0.5p = 2−p 4−p.

This result confirms the idea that the more rare characteristic C is, the closer we get to specifying which child we mean when we say that at least one of the children has C.
