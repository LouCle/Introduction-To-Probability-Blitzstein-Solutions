2.6.3 problem 62

Let D be the event that the mother has the disease. Let Ci be the event that the i-th child has the disease.

(a)
P(C1c ∩ C 2c) = P(D)P(C 1c ∩ C 2c|D) + P(Dc)P(C 1c ∩ C 2c|Dc) = 1 3 ∗ 1 4 + 2 3 = 9 12
(b)
The two events are not independent. If the elder child has the disease, the mother has the disease, which means the younger child has probability 1 2 of having the disease. Unconditionally, the younger child has probability 1 6 of having the disease.
(c)
P(D|C1c ∩ C 2c) = P(D)P(C1c ∩ C 2c|D) P(C1c ∩ C2c) = 1 3 ∗ 1 4 1 3 ∗ 1 4 + 2 3 = 1 9
